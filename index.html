01 — The Forum

A national congress, written by engineers

Successful Tunisia is a flagship multidisciplinary engineering forum organized by the IEEE ENSIT Student Branch, under the theme "Engineering, Technology & Leadership for Impact."

The forum brings together engineering students, young professionals, industry leaders and tech innovators from across Tunisia for a full day of keynote speeches, expert panels, roundtable discussions, technical challenges and an exhibition area.

It is conceived as a national platform where engineers from every discipline converge — to exchange knowledge, build networks, discover opportunities and be inspired to create impact, in Tunisia or on a global stage.

04 — The Day, Charted

Event structure

Six components, each built to deliver a distinct experience — from the opening keynote to the closing group photo.

IOpening Ceremony & Keynote＋

"The Future of Engineering in Tunisia"

A formal welcome from the organizing team and IEEE ENSIT SB leadership, followed by a keynote from a distinguished figure in engineering or technology — setting the tone for the day.

Proposed topics

  • The engineer of tomorrow: skills, mindset and adaptability
  • Tunisia's position in the global tech landscape
  • From university labs to industry: bridging the gap
IIExpert Panels＋

Panel 1 — Cybersecurity & Smart Systems

Experts from cybersecurity, IoT and smart infrastructure discuss digital security in an increasingly connected world: infrastructure resilience, device security, industrial cybersecurity and emerging career paths.

Panel 2 — Engineering Leadership in the Age of AI

How AI is reshaping the engineering profession — from stepping into leadership roles, to AI's impact across disciplines, to the ethics of AI-powered decision-making.

IIIRoundtable Discussions＋

Small, themed and moderated tables run in parallel, letting participants go deep on the topics that matter most to them.

  • Career paths: corporate vs. startup vs. freelance
  • Working abroad vs. building in Tunisia
  • How engineers become leaders — the roadmap
  • Getting your first job: what recruiters really look for
  • AI in your engineering field
  • Breaking into cybersecurity & AI
  • Success stories: engineer → CEO / founder / creator
IVTechnical Challenges＋

Teams compete across three hands-on tracks, judged by industry experts and IEEE society representatives — winners recognized at the Closing Ceremony. See the tracks below ↓

VBooths & Exhibition Area＋

A dedicated exhibition space runs throughout the day, giving participants an informal setting to meet companies and small businesses.

VIClosing Ceremony＋

Technical challenge winners announced, sponsors and speakers thanked, closing remarks from the organizing committee, and a group photo to close the book on the day.
